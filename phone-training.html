Phone Training

Learn the welcome call process, then test your knowledge and practice with real scenarios before your first calls.

Step 1 — Learn

Welcome Call Guide

1

Introduce yourself

Introduce yourself as the student's success coach and explain what that means.
  • You ensure the student gets enrolled in the proper classes
  • You support the family throughout enrollment as their main point of contact
2

Request the official transcript

Explain that the first thing needed is for the family to request the former school send the student's official transcript to records@excelhighschool.com.
  • Provide info for submitting a records request if needed
  • Mention current processing time (records processing + auditing time)
  • Let them know you'll reach out once credits are transferred — at that point the student can begin core classes
3

Learn about the student

Ask open-ended questions to understand the student's background and goals. Don't push for anything the family doesn't want to share.
  • Why did they choose Excel?
  • What are their academic goals while enrolled?
  • What are their goals or plans after graduation?

Tip: This information helps you support the student better throughout enrollment — frame it that way. Listen more than you talk.

4

Explain the credit track options

Walk through the 21.5-credit vs. 24-credit tracks and recommend one based on the student's goals — though ultimately the student and family decide.
  • The 24-credit track includes a 4th math credit (at least Pre-Calculus) and two world language credits
  • Ask whether they prefer Spanish, French, or American Sign Language
  • Assign a language in their account if appropriate
5

Recommend electives

Based on the student's interests from step 3, suggest specific electives that would be a good fit for them.
6

Summarize and close

Recap all action items for the family and set expectations for next steps.
  • E.g. contact former school for official transcript, have student reply to your email with elective preferences
  • Let them know when they can expect to hear from you next

Step 2 — Quiz & Step 3 — Role-play

Common Scenarios at Excel

For each scenario: read through the context, answer the quiz question, then role-play the call with your trainer (sit back-to-back — no visual cues!).

1
12th grade student just enrolled in a Semester program — now what?
New enrollment · Semester program
▾

Context

A 12th grade student just enrolled in the Semester program. They have a transcript from their previous school. This is your first contact with the family after enrollment is confirmed.

Quiz — What do you do first?

The student just enrolled. What is your first action on the welcome call?

Role-play

You
Answer the phone with your greeting. (Practice your opener — name, school, how can I help?)
Caller
"Hi, yes — my daughter just enrolled in the semester program. Someone told me her success coach would be reaching out?"
You
Confirm who you are and explain what a success coach does. (Keep it warm and reassuring.)
Caller
"Okay great. So what happens next? She wants to start as soon as possible."
You
Explain the transcript process, where to send it, processing time, and that she'll begin core classes once credits are transferred.
Caller
"She's really into art and music — is there anything like that?"
You
Ask open-ended questions about her interests and goals, then suggest relevant electives.
Debrief: Did you introduce yourself before jumping to logistics? Did you explain processing time clearly without creating frustration? How did the elective recommendation feel?
2
Student from NY just enrolled in the Gold Program
New enrollment · Gold Program · New York state
▾

Context

A student from New York just enrolled in the Gold Program. NY has specific graduation requirements that may differ from Excel's standard tracks. Be prepared to navigate state-specific requirements alongside Excel's credit tracks.

Quiz — Credit track recommendation

The family asks which credit track you recommend. The student plans to attend a 4-year college. What do you advise?

Role-play

Caller
"We're in New York. Does that change anything about how he'll graduate?"
You
Address the NY-specific context. (Know your state requirements or know how to find them quickly.)
Caller
"He wants to study engineering in college."
You
Use this to guide your credit track recommendation. Explain both options and why the 24-credit track fits his goals.
Caller
"He's not really a language person. Does he have to take one?"
You
Explain the world language requirement for the 24-credit track honestly. Offer the three options (Spanish, French, ASL) and note that ultimately it's their choice.
Debrief: Did you acknowledge the NY state context? Did you make a clear recommendation while respecting the family's decision? How did you handle the pushback on world language?
3
Student from CA just enrolled in the Silver Program
New enrollment · Silver Program · California state
▾

Context

A student from California just enrolled in the Silver Program. California has its own graduation requirements. This student is mid-enrollment and the family is eager to get started quickly.

Quiz — Transcript request

The parent says their student's previous school is being slow about sending the transcript. What do you tell them?

Role-play

Caller
"We're calling from California. My son just enrolled in the Silver Program. He's really eager to start — he's been out of school for a semester."
You
Introduce yourself, explain your role, and acknowledge their excitement. Set realistic expectations about the transcript process.
Caller
"His old school is taking forever. Can he just start now and sort out the credits later?"
You
Explain why the transcript review matters and what the student can do in the meantime. Keep the tone empathetic — you understand their frustration.
Caller
"He's thinking about going into nursing after graduation. Does that affect what he takes?"
You
Use this to guide science elective recommendations and the credit track conversation.
Debrief: Did you stay empathetic without making promises you can't keep? Did you use the nursing goal to shape your recommendations? How did you handle the "start now" pressure?
