Phone Training

Learn the welcome call process, then test your knowledge and practice with real scenarios before your first calls.

1

Welcome Calls

Step 1 — Learn

Welcome Call Guide

1

Introduction

Introduce yourself as the student's success coach and explain what that means.
  • You ensure the student gets enrolled in the proper classes
  • You support the family throughout enrollment as their main point of contact
2

Official Transcript — students with credits to transfer only

Explain that the family needs to request their former school send the student's official transcript to records@excelhighschool.com if they haven't already.
  • Provide information for submitting a records request if necessary
  • Mention current processing time (student records processing time + auditing time combined)
  • Let them know you'll reach out once credits are transferred — at that point the student can begin core classes
3

Student's Academic Journey

Ask open-ended questions to understand the student's background and goals. Let them know this helps you best support the student throughout enrollment. Don't push for anything the family doesn't want to share.
  • Student's reasoning for joining Excel
  • Student's academic goals while at Excel
  • Student's goals and plans for after graduation
  • Does the student plan to graduate with EHS (or move on to EHS after completing EMS)?

Tip: Ask open-ended questions and listen more than you talk. This information shapes everything else in the call.

4

Credit Track — high school students only

Explain the 21.5- vs. 24-credit tracks and advise one based on the student's goals — though ultimately the student and family decide.
  • The 24-credit track requires a 4th math credit (at least Pre-Calculus) and two world language credits
  • Ask the student's preference: Spanish, French, or American Sign Language
  • Assign a language in their account if appropriate
5

Elective Selection

Ask the student if they'd like to choose their electives.
  • Recommend specific electives based on the student's interests
  • Let Gold / Platinum / Future Ready students know they can take additional electives but are not required to
  • Make sure college-bound students are planning to take two world language electives before graduation
  • Ask Gold / Platinum / Future Ready students which core subjects they'd like as standard / honors / AP / dual enrollment
6

Conclusion

Summarize all action items the family needs to complete and let them know when to expect to hear from you.
  • E.g. contact former school for official transcript, have student reply to your email with elective preferences

Watch For

Special Cases

🌴 Student from California?

Make sure they are enrolled in the correct California-specific program.

🗽 Student from New York?

Send them the email template regarding complying with NY Department of Education requirements as a virtual student.

🎓 12th grader enrolled in a Semester program?

Direct them to enroll in a full-year program, explaining the 25% / 6-credit rule.

📅 Student young for their grade level?

Explain graduation requirements — the student must fulfill one of the following to graduate:

  • Be at least 17 years old
  • Have a college acceptance letter with a start date before their 17th birthday
  • Earn at least a 26 on the ACT or a 1270 on the SAT

Step 2 — Quiz & Step 3 — Role-play

Common Scenarios at Excel

For each scenario: read through the context, answer the quiz question, then role-play the call with your trainer (sit back-to-back — no visual cues!).

1
12th grade student just enrolled in a Semester program — now what?
New enrollment · Semester program
▾

Context

A 12th grade student just enrolled in the Semester program. They have a transcript from their previous school. This is your first contact with the family after enrollment is confirmed.

Quiz — What do you do first?

The student just enrolled. What is your first action on the welcome call?

Role-play

You
Answer the phone with your greeting. (Practice your opener — name, school, how can I help?)
Caller
"Hi, yes — my daughter just enrolled in the semester program. Someone told me her success coach would be reaching out?"
You
Confirm who you are and explain what a success coach does. (Keep it warm and reassuring.)
Caller
"Okay great. So what happens next? She wants to start as soon as possible."
You
Explain the transcript process, where to send it, processing time, and that she'll begin core classes once credits are transferred.
Caller
"She's really into art and music — is there anything like that?"
You
Ask open-ended questions about her interests and goals, then suggest relevant electives.
Debrief: Did you introduce yourself before jumping to logistics? Did you explain processing time clearly without creating frustration? How did the elective recommendation feel?

Student from NY just enrolled in the Gold Program
New enrollment · Gold Program · New York state
▾

Context

A student from New York just enrolled in the Gold Program. NY has specific graduation requirements that may differ from Excel's standard tracks. Be prepared to navigate state-specific requirements alongside Excel's credit tracks.

Quiz — Credit track recommendation

The family asks which credit track you recommend. The student plans to attend a 4-year college. What do you advise?

Role-play

Caller
"We're in New York. Does that change anything about how he'll graduate?"
You
Address the NY-specific context. (Know your state requirements or know how to find them quickly.)
Caller
"He wants to study engineering in college."
You
Use this to guide your credit track recommendation. Explain both options and why the 24-credit track fits his goals.
Caller
"He's not really a language person. Does he have to take one?"
You
Explain the world language requirement for the 24-credit track honestly. Offer the three options (Spanish, French, ASL) and note that ultimately it's their choice.
Debrief: Did you acknowledge the NY state context? Did you make a clear recommendation while respecting the family's decision? How did you handle the pushback on world language?
3
Student from CA just enrolled in the Silver Program
New enrollment · Silver Program · California state
▾

Context

A student from California just enrolled in the Silver Program. California has its own graduation requirements. This student is mid-enrollment and the family is eager to get started quickly.

Quiz — Transcript request

The parent says their student's previous school is being slow about sending the transcript. What do you tell them?

Role-play

Caller
"We're calling from California. My son just enrolled in the Silver Program. He's really eager to start — he's been out of school for a semester."
You
Introduce yourself, explain your role, and acknowledge their excitement. Set realistic expectations about the transcript process.
Caller
"His old school is taking forever. Can he just start now and sort out the credits later?"
You
Explain why the transcript review matters and what the student can do in the meantime. Keep the tone empathetic — you understand their frustration.

"He's thinking about going into nursing after graduation. Does that affect what he takes?"
You
Use this to guide science elective recommendations and the credit track conversation.
Debrief: Did you stay empathetic without making promises you can't keep? Did you use the nursing goal to shape your recommendations? How did you handle the "start now" pressure?

Inquiry & General Calls

Step 1 — Listen

Learn from a Real Call

Listen to Abby's live single course inquiry call on RingSense, then review the highlights below. Notice how she confirms the state, recommends the right course version, and is upfront about cost.

▶
Single Course Inquiry — French 2 over the summer
Live call · Abby Berndt · Parent of a CA 10th grader
▾

Call highlights

Parent
"I'm interested in having my son, who's in 10th grade, take a French 2 class over the summer. I wasn't sure about the registration deadline."
Abby
Reassures that single courses are open enrollment and self-paced with no deadline, then asks: "What state are you located in?"
Parent
"California."
Abby
Recommends the French 2 CA A-G version: UC schools have strict requirements, and it transfers more easily to a California school. Confirms the family wants the full year credit, so not the A or B half versions.
Abby
Flags the price difference upfront: "The cost for that one is $370 instead of the $325... I just want to make sure that fee does not surprise you."
Parent
"Is it something he might be able to complete over the summer?"
Abby
Very doable: about 10 hours a week over the summer. Outlines the course structure (projects, unit quizzes, midterm and final), grading turnaround (3 to 5 business days), and how the transcript is sent to the school or a future college.
Abby
Warm close: "Is there anything else I can help you with? ... Thanks for being part of our community."
What to notice: Abby asks for the state before recommending a course, steers the family to the correct CA A-G version, names the exact course so there is no confusion, surfaces the higher price before the family finds it on their own, and gives a realistic weekly time estimate instead of a vague promise.

Step 2 — Practice

Role-play Scripts

Practice each call with your trainer. Review the objectives first, then run the script. The trainer plays the caller.

1
Friendly parent asking about enrollment
Inquiry · 10th grade enrollment, program structure, grading
▾

Key objectives

  • Warm and professional greeting
  • Clearly explain the enrollment process
  • Set expectations about the self-paced model
  • Describe what is included in tuition
  • Reassure and encourage next steps

Script

Caller
"Hi, I'm looking into online school for my daughter. She'll be going into 10th grade. How do I sign her up?"
You
"Hi there! I'd be happy to help. For a 10th grader, you'd want to enroll her in our Standard High School Program unless she's interested in the Honors track. You can enroll online by clicking Enroll Now on our website, but I can walk you through it if that's easier."
Caller
"Okay. And is this a full-time program with real teachers?"
You
"Great question! It is a full high school program, but it's self-paced and asynchronous, so students work independently through the curriculum. We have certified teachers who grade and provide feedback, but there aren't live classes. Your daughter will have access to support through messaging and can request help when needed."
Caller
"What's included in the tuition?"
You
"The monthly tuition covers access to the core courses (English, Math, Science, Social Studies) and one elective at a time. Grading, transcripts, and student support are all included. There are optional fees for things like AP courses, Success Coach Pro, or course extensions if needed."
Caller
"Okay. And what about grades?"
You
"Her work is automatically graded where possible, like quizzes and multiple choice. For writing assignments and projects, our teachers grade and return them with feedback. Progress reports are available anytime in the parent portal, and final grades go on her transcript."
Caller
"That sounds good. I'll talk to my daughter and probably sign up this week."
You
"Perfect! And if you have any questions while enrolling, feel free to call us back or email. We're happy to help!"
Skills tested: Explaining enrollment, tone, pacing, explaining the asynchronous model, tuition coverage, and the support structure.
2
Concerned parent about lack of progress
Support · Frustrated caller, role clarity, progress emails
▾

Key objectives

  • Acknowledge the concern empathetically
  • Check student progress in the SIS
  • Clarify the role and limits of the Success Coach
  • Offer follow-up support and options

Script

Caller
"I'm really concerned. My son isn't making progress, and we haven't heard from his Success Coach in weeks. What's going on?"
You
"I'm so sorry to hear that, and thank you for reaching out. Can I get your son's name so I can look into his account and see what's been happening?"
Caller
"His name is Caleb Johnson."
You
"Thanks, one moment while I pull up his file... Okay, I see that Caleb is in the 11th grade program and hasn't submitted any work in about three weeks."
Caller
"That's what I'm worried about. He says he wants to finish, but he's completely unmotivated. I thought we'd be getting more support."
You
"I completely understand. It's hard to see your child struggle, especially with online learning. Our Success Coaches provide support for enrollment in courses and planning. You can also more closely monitor Caleb's progress and grades through the weekly progress emails, which are sent to the parent email on file."
Caller
"I have not been receiving those emails."
You
"Let me check the parent email on file to confirm it's correct. You can also update this through the family login. Would you like me to email you instructions for accessing the family login?"
Caller
"Yes, please. Thank you."
You
"Of course, we're here to help!"
Skills tested: Handling frustration calmly, clarifying program limits, offering a path forward, and following up with support.

Every Call

Tips for All Calls

  • Always speak with empathy and patience
  • Don't promise what you can't deliver
  • Use parent and student names to personalize the call
  • Take notes on the conversation in the CRM or ticket system
  • Offer to send a follow-up email when needed
